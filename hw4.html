This photomontage explores the experience of feeling isolated and emotionally drowned. As more and more people struggle with mental health, this issue becomes increasingly important for the public to know and understand. So I wanted to depict the feeling of being trapped, unable to escape overwhelming feelings.

To achieve this effect, I picked four images from an online site: a dark room with a window, the sea, raindrops and a silhouette. I mainly used layer masks and blending modes to create this photo. For the sea layer, I used linear light to enhance the texture of the waves. For the room layer, I used a gradient mask layer to help blend the images smoothly with the sea layer. These two layers create the feeling of being trapped, as the only way to escape was through the window but the presence of water makes it seem impossible. To integrate the silhouette, I used a gradient mask layer again to crop out unnecessary details and used a darken blend. Lastly, I added a raindrops layer by using a lighter color blend and a gradient mask. The purpose of these two layers is to enforce the idea of being trapped, as if the viewer is trapped behind a foggy surface and the silhouette is unable to escape the room.

Second collage

Photoshop screenshots

This photomontage explores the theme of economic inequality, emphasizing the gap between rich and poor in current society. "The rich get richer and the poor get poorer" has become increasingly prevalent, the widening gap between the rich and the poor is a result of many economic issues. So I wanted to create a photo that reflects the idea of the increasing gap between these two groups.

To achieve this effect, I picked five photos form an online site: a well maintained home and a worn down building, crumbled paper and ripped ends, two hands. I blended the images of the buildings using gradient masks, to create contrast and represent two distinct socio-economic backgrounds. I also blend in the crumbled paper using color burn with the worn down building to enhance the contrast and to add a layer of texture. Then I masked out the hands to symbolize the growing gap and separation of the two groups. To reinforce the idea of separation, I blended in the ripped ends using linear burn and placed it between the images of the two buildings.
